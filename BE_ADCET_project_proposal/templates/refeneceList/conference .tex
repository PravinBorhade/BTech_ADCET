\noindent{\large{\textbf{Conference Paper (Paper Presented at a Conference) }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Basic Format:  }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{J. K. Author, “Title of paper,” presented at the Abbreviated Name of Conf., City of Conf., Abbrev. State, Country, Month and day(s), year, Paper number. }
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Examples: }}} 
~\\~
\noindent{D. Caratelli, M. C. Viganó, G. Toso, and P. Angeletti, “Analytical placement technique for sparse arrays,” presented at the 32nd ESA Antenna Workshop, Noordwijk, The Netherlands, Oct. 5–8, 2010.}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Conference Proceedings in Print (Paper Presented at a Conference) }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{The general form for citing conference proceedings is to list the author and title of the paper, followed by the name of the conference in italics using standard abbreviations. Write out all the remaining words, but omit most articles and prepositions like “of the” and “on.” That is, Proceedings of the 1996 Robotics and Automation Conference becomes Proc. 1996 Robot. Automat. Conf. If an ordinal number is in the conference name, use the numerical form instead of spelling it out (e.g., “1st” instead of “First”). Include the location if given. For U.S. locations, “USA” must be included after city and state. }
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Basic Format:  }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{J. K. Author, “Title of paper,” in Abbreviated Name of Conf., (location of conference is optional), (Month and day(s) if provided) year, pp. xxx-xxx. }
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Examples:  }}}
~\\~
\noindent{A. Amador-Perez and R. A. Rodriguez-Solis, “Analysis of a CPW-fed annular slot ring antenna using DOE,” in Proc. IEEE Antennas Propag. Soc. Int. Symp., Jul. 2006, pp. 4301–4304.}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Conference Proceedings With DOI }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Basic Format:  }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{J. K. Author, “Title of paper,” in Abbreviated Name of Conf., (location of conference is optional), year, pp. xxx– xxx, doi: xxx. Examples:  G. Veruggio, “The EURON roboethics roadmap,” in Proc. Humanoids ’06: 6th IEEE-RAS Int. Conf. Humanoid Robots, 2006, pp. 612–617, doi: 10.1109/ICHR.2006.321337.  }
~\\~\\~
\noindent{J. Zhao, G. Sun, G. H. Loh, and Y. Xie, “Energy-efficient GPU design with reconfigurable in-package graphics memory,” in Proc. ACM/IEEE Int. Symp. Low Power Electron. Design (ISLPED),\newline Jul. 2012, pp. 403–408, doi: 10.1145/2333660.2333752.}
