\noindent{\large{\textbf{D. Datasets }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{The essential components of a citation to a dataset are the following: Author names of each individual or organizational entity responsible for the creation of the dataset; the complete title of the dataset, including the edition or version number, if applicable; the date published or disseminated [year and full date (if available)]; publisher and/or distributor; and electronic location or identifier (URL or DOI if applicable). Append the date retrieved if the title and locator are not specific to the exact instance of the data you used. }
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Basic Format:  }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{Author, “Title.” (Date, Year). Distributed by Publisher/Distributor. http://url.com (or if DOI is used, end with a period) Humanoid }
~\\~\\~
\noindent{\large{\textbf{Example:  }}}
~\\~\\~
\noindent{S. Ansolabehere, M. Palmer, and A. Lee. “Precinct-level election data. V1.” January 20, 2014. Distributed by Harvard Election Data Archive.\newline
http://hdl.handle.net/1902.1/21919 UNF:5:5C9UfGjdLy2ONVPtgr45qA==}
